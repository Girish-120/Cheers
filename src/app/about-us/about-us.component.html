The Cheers Story

From founder, Cardia Summers

As a young child growing up in Hopkinsville, Kentucky the notion of attending an Ivy League school was preposterous. Never in a million years would I have thought of I would attend a college, let alone one of Columbia University’s caliber.

As the product of a single-parent household, my mother struggled with drug addiction. My father was my caregiver. A career soldier, his service made me the perpetual “new guy” at school every two to three years to include three different high schools.

Luckily, I always felt as if I could forge my own path and overcome obstacles to achieve my own vision of success. I found my calling in the Army. For a decade, I served as a paralegal assistant with some of the military’s finest attorneys. I even became a Ranger to serve with the best special operations forces in the world.

My career was on the rise after winning both Soldier and Noncommissioned Officer of the Year competitions as a junior associate and a mid-grade manager. Then, I was selected as the Army’s Paralegal of the Year. Needless to say, I was following in my father’s footsteps on a path to achieve a high enlisted rank in the military.

One bad choice ruined that dream. A drinking and driving offense became the only blemish on my 10-year career. For that bad decision, my career ended.

If I learned anything as a Ranger, it was to take our lessons learned on the battlefield and apply them for the betterment of our team and our community. My lumps became my motivation to continue to excel at Columbia and with Cheers.

Cheers is a product of my redemption. Instead of bemoaning my situation, I created a service to reduce drinking and driving.

Whether a wedding, birthday, or a house party, Cheers cater to your desire for the party to go in a responsible manner. There is no need to leave the safety of your home to purchase more adult beverages. Allow Cheers to deliver your needs in a responsible manner.

I struggled for years to tell my story. Now, I stand a little taller by working to solve a known problem with social drinking. Here’s to the memories we will help create for others by first learning for ourselves. Cheers!
